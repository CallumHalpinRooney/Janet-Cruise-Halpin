About Phil

Phil McDarby is a freelance digital artist, photographer & composer, living and working in Dublin, Ireland.

He has been drawing and painting fantasy and sci-fi characters and environments for most of his life, and made the jump from traditional to digital around 1998. At the heart of his work is a desire to capture a sense of magic and wonder — that feeling of child-like excitement and discovery that we can lose touch with as adults.

That instinct goes back to childhood — to Enid Blyton's Faraway Tree, and to real woods a short walk from home. He has spent his life since chasing the same feeling: the sense that, just beyond our sight, an ancient wood is quietly full of dragons and spirits.

Much of the work belongs to The World of the Wood Dragon — an invented mythology of forest dragons, a dozen species now, each one grown from real bark, moss and macro photography and painted into being. Alongside it he keeps A Daily Forest, an ongoing photographic return to the woods themselves.

He uses a number of methods to create his imagery, from 2D painting using a Wacom tablet to 3D modelling in Maya, Bryce and 3D Studio Max, from digital sculpting in ZBrush to matte work using his own photography as textures in his scenes.

He has won over 50 international awards, including multiple CG Choice Awards from the CGSociety and the Golden Torus Award. His work has featured in many publications, including Exposé 6 and 7 from Ballistic Media and many international magazines, and he has composed music for film and television.

In 2001, he joined MediaLab Europe as a Research Associate, where he worked on numerous games — modelling and animating the game assets and environments, designing the sound effects, and composing the soundtracks. In 2004, he left MediaLab Europe to co-found Vyro Games, where for five years he oversaw the creative process while taking a hands-on role in all aspects of content production. In 2009, he was accepted into the Illustrators Guild of Ireland.

He now works as a freelance concept artist and illustrator, and is available for commissions — get in touch.
