The Artist

Grounded in the wild

Janet Cruise Halpin (b.1952) is a renowned Irish artist living and working in Laragh, Co. Wicklow — in the heart of the Wicklow Mountains. Her studio overlooks the ancient oak woodland of Glendalough, and that deep sense of place infuses every canvas.

Working primarily in oil on canvas, Janet builds rich surface layers — ochre light through a forest canopy, the hush of morning mist over a lake, the muscular weight of stone. Her paintings have been exhibited widely across Ireland and are held in private collections internationally.

The voice
behind the canvas

"I am drawn to the moment when light falls through leaves — not the leaves themselves, but what happens between them. The Wicklow forests hold a particular quality of green that I have spent thirty years trying to capture. I am still trying."
